Introduction to Dr. Jose Rizal

Jose Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda was born in Calamba, Laguna, on June 19, 1861. Rizal was the seventh son of the eleven children of Don Francisco Mercado and Doña Teodora Alonso, who were both educated. He has a lot of talents, including sketching, painting, sculpture, and literature. His first poem was written when he was 8 years old. He used to study at Biñan Laguna, Ateneo Municipal, and the University of Sto. Tomas.

Journey



Dr. Jose Rizal, the national hero of the Philippines, had a remarkable journey throughout his life. He was born on June 19, 1861, in Calamba, Laguna, and grew up in a loving and educated family. Rizal's thirst for knowledge led him to pursue higher education in Manila, Spain, Germany, and France. During his time abroad, Rizal immersed himself in various fields of study, including medicine, literature, and the sciences. He became fluent in several languages and developed a deep understanding of different cultures and societies. Rizal's experiences abroad greatly influenced his perspective on colonialism and social injustice, which would later shape his advocacy for Philippine independence. Rizal's most notable work, "Noli Me Tangere" (Touch Me Not), exposed the harsh realities of Spanish colonial rule in the Philippines. This novel sparked a sense of national consciousness among Filipinos and ignited the flames of revolution against Spanish oppression. Upon returning to the Philippines, Rizal continued to fight for reforms and equality through his writings and peaceful activism. However, his efforts were met with resistance from the Spanish authorities. In 1896, Rizal was unjustly accused of rebellion and sentenced to death by firing squad. Despite his tragic end, Rizal's legacy lives on as a symbol of courage, patriotism, and intellectual brilliance. His writings and ideals continue to inspire generations of Filipinos to strive for freedom, justice, and national pride.

Summary Of Rizal's Books



"Noli Me Tangere"




"Noli Me Tangere" (Touch Me Not): Published in 1887, this novel is considered Rizal's most famous work. It exposes the social injustices and abuses committed by the Spanish colonial government in the Philippines. The story follows the life of Crisostomo Ibarra, a young Filipino who returns to his homeland after studying in Europe, and his encounters with various characters that represent different aspects of Philippine society.

"El Filibusterismo"




"El Filibusterismo" (The Reign of Greed): Published in 1891, this sequel to "Noli Me Tangere" delves deeper into the themes of revolution and social inequality. It continues the story of Crisostomo Ibarra, who has transformed into the mysterious and vengeful character known as Simoun. Through the narrative, Rizal criticizes the oppressive Spanish regime and calls for reform.

"Mi Ultimo Adios"




"Mi Ultimo Adios" (My Last Farewell): This poem, written by Rizal on the eve of his execution in 1896, expresses his love for his country and his willingness to sacrifice his life for its freedom. It serves as a poignant farewell and a call to action for future generations of Filipinos.
